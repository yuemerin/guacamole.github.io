Overview of Pharmacology in Nursing:

  • Study of drugs and their interactions with living organisms.
  • Integral part of nursing practice for patient care and symptom management.
  • Focuses on drug administration, effects, side effects, and patient education.

Nursing Considerations:

  • Assessment of patient's medical history and medication allergies.
  • Administration of medications via various routes (oral, intravenous, intramuscular).
  • Monitoring for therapeutic effects and potential adverse reactions.
  • Providing patient education on medication adherence and potential side effects.

Pharmacokinetics:

  • Absorption: How drugs enter the bloodstream.
  • Distribution: How drugs travel to target tissues.
  • Metabolism: How drugs are broken down in the body.
  • Excretion: How drugs are eliminated from the body.

Common Medication Classes:

  • Antibiotics: Treat bacterial infections.
  • Analgesics: Relieve pain.
  • Anticoagulants: Prevent blood clot formation.
  • Antihypertensives: Lower blood pressure.

Collaboration with Other Healthcare Professionals:

  • Collaborating with physicians for medication prescription and adjustments.
  • Consulting with pharmacists for medication information and interactions.
  • Working with clinical pharmacologists for specialized drug knowledge.
